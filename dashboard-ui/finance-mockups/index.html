Atlas Dashboard / Finance Tab Redesign

Finance tab rewrite — 8 design variants

Two rounds of parallel prototypes. Round 1 (A–D below) took four different strong opinions about how to answer "where are my savings at?" Round 2 (B1–B4) evolves the chosen direction (Goal Buckets) with a sharper focus on making overspend obvious. All variants consume an identical mock dataset modelled on the Up Bank API (named savers with goals, round-ups with boost, HELD vs SETTLED, 2Up joint accounts, cashback, foreign amounts) so the comparison is about design, not data.

Variant A

Savings Story

Tell me in plain English how I'm doing. One sentence, then back it up.

Narrow column layout like a newspaper briefing. Serif headline with the month's headline number embedded in prose ("You saved $4,240 in May, putting you on pace for $215K by year end"). A short prose paragraph synthesises what changed — categories, round-ups, joint account split. Saver progress as wide horizontal bars. Tabular "by the numbers" appendix at the bottom. Zero panels above the fold, no big charts — just words and one tiny net-worth sparkline.

Best if you only check the tab once a week and want a written briefing that tells you the story without making you read charts.

Variant B

Goal Buckets

Savings are buckets filling up. Show me each pot and when it'll be full.

The four Up savers (Emergency Fund, House Deposit, Tax, Bali) shown as tangible vertical "liquid-fill" buckets, each with current $, target $, %, ETA at current pace, and monthly contribution. A dedicated round-up routing callout shows "$47.83 → House Deposit". An emergency-fund runway scale highlights the 3/6/12-month thresholds. Spending compressed to a single stacked bar. Milestone timeline along the bottom. Monzo Pots / YNAB DNA.

Best if your mental model of money is "named pots with goals" and you want the dashboard to mirror how you actually think about saving.

Goal-first Pot visuals ETA-driven
Variant C

Cash Flow

Show me where every dollar of income actually goes.

A large hand-rolled SVG Sankey diagram dominates the page: Income → Fixed / Variable / Saved → named savers and top categories. Path widths proportional to dollar amounts; savings streams in green. Round-ups appear as their own sub-stream into House Deposit. Below the flow, a savings-rate gauge, a 6-month savings-rate trend, and a HELD-vs-SETTLED split. Joint-account split shown as a small horizontal panel. Answers "where does my money leak?" instantly.

Best if you want to see the structure of your finances at a glance and immediately spot where money is or isn't reaching your savers.

Sankey-first Single hero visual Diagnostic
Variant D

Calm Minimal

Three numbers. Net worth, saved this month, runway. That's the whole tab.

The most extreme variant. Three massive numbers stacked with generous whitespace. Below them, one sentence: "On pace to reach $200K by August 2026." A single thin net-worth sparkline. A monospaced 4-line list of savers (current / target / % / ETA). Everything else lives behind a "Show details" disclosure: spending split, recurring annual, round-up boost, cashback YTD, joint split. Apple Wallet / iA Writer levels of restraint.

Best if you check the tab on your phone in 10 seconds and the current dashboard's density actively stresses you out.

3 numbers No panels Disclosure-driven

Variant B evolutions — overspend focus

You picked Goal Buckets as the direction and asked for four sibling designs that keep the saver-bucket DNA but each take a different strong opinion about making overspend obvious. Same shared dataset; same accent palette for the savers; the overspend mechanic is what differs.

Variant B1

Bucket Pressure

Every spending bucket has a pressure gauge. Green → amber → red as you approach the limit.

Keeps the saver buckets but ADDS a row of "pressure tiles" — one per spending account / budget — where each shows current vs limit on a continuous green→amber→red bar with a projection pin marking "where you'll land at this pace". A dedicated overspend forecast panel shows the month-end gap to budget. Category trend rows below highlight which categories are leaking month-over-month.

Best if you want one glance to tell you "are any of my budgets about to blow up" before they actually do.

Traffic-light Projection pin Category drift
Variant B2

Save Up / Bleed Out

Top half saves up. Bottom half bleeds out. Overspent categories visibly overflow.

Vertical mirror. Top half: the 4 saver pots filling upward (positive flow, green). Bottom half: 4 INVERTED leaky buckets for spending categories — when over budget, the bucket visibly overflows with a red puddle SVG below it. Twin commentary panels read "what you saved" (left) vs "what leaked" (right). Connects an overspending category to the saver delay it causes in plain prose.

Best if you respond to visceral visual metaphors — seeing money spill out of a bucket lands harder than a number going red.

Mirror layout Overflow viz Editorial
Variant B3

Daily Allowance

Reframe everything in daily units. "You've got $74 to spend today without missing your goal."

A big circular ring shows TODAY's discretionary allowance as it depletes — like a phone health widget. A 30-day calendar heatmap shows your overspend pattern at a glance (red cells = days you blew the daily budget). Saver buckets compress to a horizontal strip with each contribution reframed as "$X/day". A what-if slider lets you test "if I cut $30/day, my House Deposit fills 6 weeks sooner".

Best if you find monthly totals abstract but "$74 left today" feels concrete and stops you mid-tap at checkout.

Health-ring Calendar heatmap What-if slider
Variant B4

Burn-down Mountain

The month is a budget you're burning down. Areas above the budget line fill red.

A large hand-rolled SVG mountain shows month-to-date spend vs the straight-line budget. Where the actual line crosses above budget, the difference is filled red; where it stays under, that gap is filled green — that green cushion is what flows into your savers. A dashed projection wedge extends to month-end. Per-category mini burn-downs and a 6-month history bar chart give you the diary of your overspend tendency over time.

Best if you think in trajectories — seeing the actual line creep toward the budget line tells you the story before the month is over.

Up Bank API features surfaced in these mockups

  • Named savers as goal-bearing accounts — each saver has a target, ETA, monthly contribution, and 5.10% interest rate.
  • Round-ups with boost portion — $47.83 captured this month at 2× boost, routed to House Deposit saver.
  • HELD vs SETTLED transactions — pending transactions shown distinctly from settled.
  • 2Up joint account with performingCustomer — Jordan vs Partner spend split on the shared account.
  • Cashback as a separate income stream — small recurring credit visible in the flow.
  • Foreign currency amounts — original USD value preserved alongside AUD.
  • cardPurchaseMethod — contactless / ecommerce / direct-debit split available for analysis.

All four prototypes are self-contained HTML with embedded CSS/JS and a shared mock dataset. No build step, no backend, no React. They are design references — pick the direction that resonates, and the chosen variant gets reimplemented as React components in dashboard-ui/src/components/finance/ against the real /api/finance endpoint.

Try opening two in side-by-side browser windows to compare directly. Don't pick on aesthetics alone — pick on which information architecture actually answers "where are my savings" for the way you think.
